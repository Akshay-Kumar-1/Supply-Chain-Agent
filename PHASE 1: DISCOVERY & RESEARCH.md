## 1. Industry Overview 

  

### Supply Chain Management Software Market 

- Global market size: ~$28.9B (2025), projected $50.2B by 2030 

- CAGR: ~11.7% 

- Key growth drivers: AI adoption, post-COVID resilience focus, digital 

  transformation mandates, ESG/sustainability tracking requirements 

  

### SMB Segment Specifically 

- SMBs represent ~60% of global businesses but only ~15% of SCM software 

  spend 

- Primary barrier: cost and complexity of existing solutions 

- 78% of SMBs still use spreadsheets as their primary supply chain tool 

  (Gartner SMB Survey, 2025) 

- Only 12% of SMBs have adopted any AI tool for operations 

  

## 2. Key Market Trends 

  

### Trend 1: AI Agent Paradigm Shift 

The market is moving from "AI as a feature" (chatbot on a dashboard) to 

"AI as an agent" (autonomous system that takes actions). Major vendors 

(SAP, Oracle, Microsoft) announced agent-based supply chain features in 

2025, but all target enterprise customers. 

  

### Trend 2: Composable Supply Chain 

Gartner's "composable supply chain" framework — modular, interchangeable 

components rather than monolithic suites — aligns perfectly with our 

multi-agent architecture. SMBs want to adopt modules incrementally. 

  

### Trend 3: Democratization of Data Analytics 

Tools like ChatGPT, Claude, and Copilot have normalized the idea of 

"asking questions to your data." Supply chain teams now expect to be able 

to query their data conversationally, not through complex BI tools. 

  

### Trend 4: Supply Chain Resilience as Board-Level Priority 

Post-COVID, post-Suez, post-semiconductor-shortage — supply chain risk 

management has moved from operational concern to strategic imperative. 

Even SMBs are investing in visibility and early warning systems. 

  

## 3. Customer Segments Analysis 

  

### Segment A: Small Manufacturers (50-200 employees) 

- Revenue: $5M-$30M 

- SKUs: 50-500 

- Suppliers: 10-50 

- Current tools: Excel, basic accounting software (Tally/QuickBooks) 

- Pain: No visibility across operations, reactive purchasing 

- Willingness to pay: $200-$500/month 

- Decision maker: Owner/GM or Operations Manager 

  

### Segment B: Mid-Size Distributors (100-500 employees) 

- Revenue: $20M-$100M 

- SKUs: 200-2,000 

- Suppliers: 20-100 

- Current tools: Basic ERP (Odoo, ERPNext), lots of Excel alongside 

- Pain: ERP doesn't provide intelligence, too much manual reporting 

- Willingness to pay: $500-$2,000/month 

- Decision maker: VP Supply Chain or COO 

  

### Segment C: E-Commerce Operations (10-200 employees) 

- Revenue: $2M-$50M 

- SKUs: 100-5,000 

- Suppliers: 10-200 (often international) 

- Current tools: Shopify/WooCommerce + Excel + Google Sheets 

- Pain: Demand volatility, stockouts = lost sales, complex fulfillment 

- Willingness to pay: $200-$1,000/month 

- Decision maker: Founder, Head of Ops 

  

### Primary Target: Segments A and C (lowest barrier to entry, highest 

pain, most Excel-dependent) 

  

## 4. Buying Behavior 

  

- SMBs prefer monthly subscriptions over annual contracts 

- Free trial or freemium model is expected 

- Time-to-value must be < 1 week (ideally < 1 day for first insight) 

- Word-of-mouth and LinkedIn content are primary discovery channels 

- Case studies and ROI calculators are key conversion tools 

- Decision cycle: 1-4 weeks (much shorter than enterprise)

## Competitive Matrix 

  

### Tier 1: Enterprise SCM Suites (Indirect Competitors) 

  

#### SAP Integrated Business Planning (IBP) 

- **What:** Enterprise-grade supply chain planning suite 

- **Strengths:** Comprehensive, deep ERP integration, trusted brand 

- **Weaknesses:** 6-18 month implementation, $500K+ cost, requires 

  consultants, not accessible to SMBs 

- **AI features:** Launched "Joule" AI assistant (2025) but limited to 

  enterprise customers 

- **Threat level:** Low (different market segment entirely) 

- **Our counter:** We serve the 90% of businesses they'll never touch 

  

#### Oracle SCM Cloud 

- **What:** Cloud-based supply chain management suite 

- **Strengths:** Strong logistics and procurement, Oracle ecosystem 

- **Weaknesses:** Complex, expensive, enterprise-focused 

- **AI features:** Embedded ML for demand sensing 

- **Threat level:** Low 

- **Our counter:** Same as SAP — different universe 

  

#### Kinaxis RapidResponse 

- **What:** Concurrent planning platform for supply chains 

- **Strengths:** Scenario planning, what-if analysis, speed 

- **Weaknesses:** Mid-to-enterprise pricing ($100K+/year), complex setup 

- **AI features:** "Maestro" AI engine for autonomous planning 

- **Threat level:** Medium-low (could move downmarket eventually) 

- **Our counter:** Speed to value, price point, no-code approach 

  

### Tier 2: SMB Supply Chain Tools (Direct Competitors) 

  

#### Inventoro 

- **What:** AI-powered inventory optimization for SMBs 

- **Strengths:** Purpose-built for small businesses, affordable 

- **Weaknesses:** Single module (inventory only), no risk management, 

  no supplier scoring, no agent architecture 

- **Pricing:** $99-$499/month 

- **Threat level:** High (closest competitor in SMB space) 

- **Our counter:** 7 modules vs. 1, AI agent reasoning vs. statistical 

  rules, persona-based outputs 

  

#### Cin7 (formerly TradeGecko) 

- **What:** Inventory and order management platform 

- **Strengths:** Good e-commerce integrations, user-friendly 

- **Weaknesses:** No AI/ML capabilities, no forecasting, no risk module 

- **Pricing:** $349-$999/month 

- **Threat level:** Medium 

- **Our counter:** AI-native vs. traditional software 

  

#### Zoho Inventory 

- **What:** Part of Zoho suite, inventory management 

- **Strengths:** Affordable, Zoho ecosystem integration 

- **Weaknesses:** Basic features, no AI, no supply chain intelligence 

- **Pricing:** $79-$299/month 

- **Threat level:** Medium (on price alone) 

- **Our counter:** Intelligence layer they completely lack 

  

#### Netstock 

- **What:** Inventory and demand planning for mid-market 

- **Strengths:** Good forecasting, ERP integrations 

- **Weaknesses:** More expensive, requires ERP, no risk/supplier modules 

- **Pricing:** $1,000-$5,000/month 

- **Threat level:** Medium 

- **Our counter:** No ERP required, broader scope, lower cost 

  

### Tier 3: AI/Automation Tools (Adjacent Competitors) 

  

#### Generic AI (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini used manually) 

- **What:** Users upload spreadsheets and ask questions manually 

- **Strengths:** Flexible, already adopted, free/cheap 

- **Weaknesses:** No automation, no memory between sessions, no workflow, 

  requires prompt engineering skill 

- **Threat level:** High (biggest "competitor" is the DIY approach) 

- **Our counter:** Automated workflows, persistent memory, structured 

  outputs, no prompt engineering needed 

  

#### Zapier / Make.com with AI 

- **What:** Generic automation platforms with AI nodes 

- **Strengths:** Flexible, large integration library 

- **Weaknesses:** Not purpose-built for supply chain, requires significant 

  setup, no domain expertise in prompts 

- **Threat level:** Medium 

- **Our counter:** Pre-built domain-specific workflows and prompts

# User Interview Scripts 

  

## Interview Protocol 

  

### Logistics 

- **Duration:** 45-60 minutes 

- **Format:** Video call (Zoom/Google Meet), recorded with consent 

- **Incentive:** $50 Amazon gift card or 3 months free access to MVP 

- **Sample size target:** 15-20 interviews across all personas 

  

### General Guidelines 

- Use open-ended questions; avoid leading 

- Let silence work — don't rush to fill gaps 

- Follow up with "Tell me more about that" and "Why?" 

- Take notes on emotions and frustrations, not just facts 

- Record exact quotes for insight synthesis 

  

--- 

  

## Script A: Supply Chain Manager / Operations Lead 

  

### Warm-Up (5 minutes) 

1. Tell me about your role and your team. 

2. How long have you been managing supply chain operations? 

3. What does a typical day/week look like for you? 

  

### Current State & Pain Points (15 minutes) 

4. Walk me through how you currently manage your supply chain — from 

   forecasting demand to getting products delivered. 

5. What tools do you use today? (Probe: Excel, ERP, email, WhatsApp, etc.) 

6. What's the most frustrating part of your current process? 

7. Tell me about the last time a supply chain problem caught you off guard. 

   What happened? What was the impact? 

8. How do you currently forecast demand? How accurate is it? 

9. How do you decide when and how much to reorder? 

10. How do you track supplier performance? Is it systematic or ad-hoc? 

  

### Decision Making & Information Flow (10 minutes) 

11. When a critical issue happens (stockout, supplier delay, quality 

    problem), how do you find out about it? 

12. How long does it typically take from "problem detected" to "action 

    taken"? 

13. Who else in your organization needs supply chain information? How do 

    they get it today? 

14. What reports or dashboards do you create? How often? How long does 

    it take? 

  

### Aspirations & Willingness to Change (10 minutes) 

15. If you had a magic wand, what would you change about how your supply 

    chain operates? 

16. Have you tried any AI tools for your work? What was the experience? 

17. If an AI system could automate parts of your supply chain management, 

    which parts would you want automated first? 

18. What would make you nervous about AI making supply chain decisions? 

19. How much would you be willing to pay per month for a tool that could 

    reduce stockouts by 50% and save you 10 hours/week? 

  

### Wrap-Up (5 minutes) 

20. Is there anything else about your supply chain challenges that I 

    should know? 

21. Would you be interested in being a beta tester for our product? 

22. Can you recommend anyone else I should talk to? 

  

--- 

  

## Script B: Procurement Lead 

  

### Warm-Up (5 minutes) 

1. Tell me about your procurement role and responsibilities. 

2. How many suppliers do you manage? How many SKUs? 

  

### Current Process Deep-Dive (15 minutes) 

3. Walk me through how a purchase order gets created today — from the 

   moment someone says "we need to buy X" to the PO being sent. 

4. How do you select which supplier to use for a given purchase? 

5. How do you compare quotes? What factors matter most? 

6. How do you track whether suppliers deliver on time and at quality? 

7. What's your biggest headache in procurement? 

8. Tell me about a time a supplier let you down. What happened? 

   How did you find out? 

  

### Supplier Management (10 minutes) 

9. Do you have a formal supplier scoring or rating system? 

10. How often do you review supplier performance? 

11. How do you identify backup suppliers? Do you have them for critical 

    items? 

12. Have you ever been surprised by a supplier risk (financial trouble, 

    natural disaster)? How did you respond? 

  

### Automation Appetite (10 minutes) 

13. What percentage of your time is spent on manual, repetitive tasks 

    (data entry, follow-up emails, creating POs)? 

14. If you could automate one part of your job, what would it be? 

15. How would you feel about an AI system drafting POs for your approval? 

16. What information would you need to see to trust an AI's supplier 

    recommendation? 

  

### Wrap-Up (5 minutes) 

17. What does "great" procurement look like to you? 

18. Interested in beta testing? 

19. Anyone else I should speak with? 

  

--- 

  

## Script C: Logistics Coordinator 

  

### Warm-Up (5 minutes) 

1. Tell me about your role in logistics/shipping operations. 

2. What types of shipments do you handle? (inbound, outbound, both) 

  

### Current Process (15 minutes) 

3. How do you track shipments today? What tools do you use? 

4. How do you find out when a shipment is delayed? 

5. What do you do when a shipment is delayed or lost? 

6. How do you choose carriers? Is it pre-negotiated or ad-hoc? 

7. What's the most stressful part of your job? 

8. Tell me about a logistics disaster — what happened, how did you 

   handle it? 

  

### Information & Communication (10 minutes) 

9. How do you communicate shipment status to internal teams? 

10. How often do you have to manually check carrier websites for updates? 

11. What information do you wish you had earlier or faster? 

  

### Automation & AI (10 minutes) 

12. If a system could predict delays 2-3 days in advance, how would 

    that change your work? 

13. What actions would you want automated vs. what do you want to 

    control manually? 

14. Would you trust an AI to reroute a shipment automatically? 

    Under what conditions? 

  

### Wrap-Up (5 minutes) 

15. What would make your job significantly easier? 

16. Beta testing interest? 

  

--- 

  

## Script D: C-Suite / VP Operations 

  

### Warm-Up (5 minutes) 

1. Tell me about your role and what supply chain means to your business. 

2. How much of your revenue is impacted by supply chain performance? 

  

### Strategic View (15 minutes) 

3. What are your top 3 supply chain challenges right now? 

4. How visible is your supply chain to you today? Rate 1-10. 

5. What supply chain metrics do you track? How often? 

6. How do you currently get supply chain updates? (Reports, meetings, 

   ad-hoc calls?) 

7. Tell me about a supply chain disruption that hit your bottom line. 

   What was the financial impact? 

  

### Decision Making (10 minutes) 

8. What information do you need to make better supply chain decisions? 

9. How much time does your team spend preparing reports for you? 

10. If you could see one dashboard every morning, what would be on it? 

  

### Investment & ROI (10 minutes) 

11. What have you invested in for supply chain tools? What was the ROI? 

12. What would justify a new supply chain tool investment? 

13. If an AI tool could reduce stockouts by 50%, cut procurement time 

    by 60%, and give you a daily risk briefing — what would that be 

    worth to you? 

14. What's your budget range for supply chain technology? 

  

### Wrap-Up (5 minutes) 

15. What keeps you up at night about your supply chain? 

16. Beta testing interest?
